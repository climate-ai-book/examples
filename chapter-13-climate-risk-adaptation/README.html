Key Concepts and Mathematical Foundations

1. Fundamental Climate Risk Equation

Integrated Risk Framework:

Risk = f(Hazard, Exposure, Vulnerability) = ∫_Ω H(x,t)·E(x)·V(x,s) dxWhere:

H: hazard intensity at location x, time t
E: exposure (people, assets, ecosystems)
V: vulnerability (socioeconomic characteristics s)
Ω: spatial domain

2. Machine Learning Risk Assessment

Neural Network Decomposition:

Risk_ML(x,t) = f_neural(H_climate(x,t), E_population(x), E_infrastructure(x), 
                        V_socioeconomic(x), V_adaptive(x))Components:

Climate hazard inputs
Population and infrastructure exposure
Baseline socioeconomic vulnerability
Adaptive capacity

3. Flood Risk Formulation

Probabilistic Damage Integration:

R_flood(x) = ∫₀^∞ P(h > h₀)·D(h,x)·A_exposed(x) dhWhere:

P(h > h₀): probability of depth h exceeding threshold
D(h,x): depth-damage function
A_exposed: exposed assets

4. Temporal Risk Dynamics

Risk Evolution Components:

dRisk/dt = (∂H/∂t)|_climate · E · V + H · (∂E/∂t)|_development + H · E · (∂V/∂t)|_adaptationThree drivers:

Hazard trends from climate change
Exposure trends from development
Vulnerability changes from adaptation

5. Flood Susceptibility Mapping

Multi-Factor Ensemble:

S_flood(x) = f_ensemble(T_topography(x), H_hydrology(x), M_meteorology(x), 
                        L_landuse(x), S_soil(x))Conditioning factors:

Topographic indices (slope, elevation, TWI)
Hydrological characteristics (drainage, distance to river)
Meteorological patterns (precipitation, intensity)
Land use and soil properties

6. Hybrid Flood Modeling

Physics + ML Integration:

h_hybrid(x,t) = h_physical(x,t; p) + Δh_ML(x,t; X_local)Combines:

Physics-based hydraulic model (parameters p)
ML corrections for local features

7. Reinforcement Learning Adaptation

Optimal Policy Under Uncertainty:

π*(s_t) = argmax_π E[Σₜ γᵗ R(s_t, a_t, s_{t+1}) | π]Where:

s_t: system state (flood risk conditions)
a_t: adaptation actions
R: reward (protection benefits - costs)
γ: discount factor

8. Coastal Adaptation RL

Multi-Strategy Optimization:

a*_coastal(t) = π_RL(h_SLR(t), P_storm(t), E_exposure(t), B_budget(t), I_existing(t))Actions optimize:

Gray infrastructure (seawalls, levees)
Green infrastructure (wetlands, dunes)
Policy measures (building codes, retreat)

9. Multi-Objective Adaptation

Composite Reward Function:

R_multi(s,a) = w^T · [R_flood_protection, -C_infrastructure, R_ecosystem, 
                      -E_carbon, R_equity]^TBalances:

Flood protection benefits
Infrastructure costs
Ecosystem services
Carbon footprint
Equity considerations

10. Infrastructure Resilience

Robustness + Recovery:

R_infrastructure = ∫₀^t_recovery Q(t)/Q₀ dt + λ_redundancy·D_redundancy 
                   - λ_cascade·P_cascadeMetrics:

Q(t): functionality over time
D_redundancy: system redundancy
P_cascade: cascade failure probability

11. Graph Networks for Infrastructure

Network Topology Modeling:

F_i^(l+1) = σ(W^(l)·F_i^(l) + Σ_{j∈N(i)} W_edge^(l)·E_ij·F_j^(l))Components:

Node features F_i
Edge attributes E_ij (connection strength)
Neighbor aggregation

12. Financial Climate Risk

Physical + Transition Risk:

FinRisk_climate = PhysicalRisk(H_hazards, A_assets) + TransitionRisk(P_policy, T_technology)Credit Risk Integration:
P_default(i,t) = f_ML(F_financial(i,t), R_climate(i,t), S_sector(i), G_geography(i))

13. Supply Chain Resilience

Climate-Aware Design:

min_x [C_operation(x) + E_climate[C_disruption(x, D_climate)]]Optimizes:

Operational costs
Expected disruption costs under climate scenarios

14. Adaptation Pathway Scoring

Multi-Criteria Analysis:

Score_adaptation(a) = Σⱼ wⱼ·Cⱼ(a)Criteria:

Effectiveness
Cost
Co-benefits
Equity
Flexibility
